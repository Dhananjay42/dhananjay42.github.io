Hey! 

I am Dhananjay, currently doing my MS in Computational & Mathematical Engineering (ICME) at Stanford University. Before this, I completed an Interdisciplinary Dual Degree (IDDD) from IIT Madras, where I majored in Mechanical Engineering and Data Science while minoring in Mathematics. 

My past research experience has been predominantly in Computer Vision, with our work on Crack Detection and Segmentation being accepted as part of the CVCIE (Computer Vision for Civil & Infrastructure Engineering) Workshop at ECCV (European Conference for Computer Vision) 2022. 

More recently, I’ve been drawn to applying STEM, machine learning, and statistics within the social sciences to address issues of disparity and inequity. While I’m still exploring specific directions, areas I’m particularly interested in include:

- AI Bias
- Decolonial/Non-Anglocentric AI
- Causal Inference
- Geo-Spatial Data Science
- STEM in Governance

Through my work, I aim to bring a firmly anti-imperial and anti-caste perspective, with the long-term goal of returning to India to use data and mathematical tools in service of identifying and eradicating class and caste disparities.
